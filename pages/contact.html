Background

I am a Software Developer living and working Bellingham, WA. I'm constantly looking for projects that are at the intersection of math, art, and technology. The Seattle Math Museum and the Illustrating Mathematics community has helped to inspire and support me in my endeavors, where I continue to pursue my interest in making mathematics accessible and engaging to broader audiences.

Why I Built This

This project started as a personal experiment in creating bad math art. I loved creating math art as a Masters student and instructor but had a tough time during my PhD where I stopped making things altogether. This project was rebelling against that feeling and going back to creating math art, for the enjoyment of it, in any shape or form. I gave a presentation at ICERM through the Illustrating Math community where I give more reasons to create: bad math art.

The project consists of a 16 by 16 led version of the Arnold Cat map, using an ESP32 microcontroller to animate the encryption process.
